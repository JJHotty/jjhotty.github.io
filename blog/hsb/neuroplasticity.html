Neuroplasticity...

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.
Knowing the principles of Neuroplasticity helps someone develop methods of checking in on their own learning development. They are able to focus on pairing their new knowledge with previously known content. And they are able to utilise the knowledge by understanding that they too are capable of knowing more and learning more. Creating a culture of learning rather than frustration and giving up.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit.
I think the way I’d like to engage with the principles is by being excited by the knowledge that I have gained. I’d also like to work on pairing new content with previously learned content so that I can make bigger and better neural highways.

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?
By spacing content and coming back to it. Also by utilising the learned knowledge over and over.
Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.
https://www.verywellmind.com/what-is-brain-plasticity-2794886

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.
Growth mindset is a psychologically explored concept that there are two main mindsets. One being fixed. The idea that the person that holds this mindset thinks that they are incapable of growing, or don’t want to. If a challenge comes up, they struggle to utilise their brain and it doesn’t go into a solution mode.. rather an escape or cheat my way out of this. But a growth mindset is the opposite, and when a challenge comes their is a level of excitement, but also acceptance that this is hard, but I’ll be able to learn and grow from this experience.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?
I think upon reflection there have been moments in my life where I have found myself in a fixed mindset, and study has been one that I’ll be willing so far, until I have proved to myself in some way that I’m not good enough, or don’t measure up. It’s at that point that I want to flick the switch and stay in a growth mindset- Reminding myself that I am capable. And I can learn and grow through this.

How will you integrate growth mindset into your learning journey?
As above- I’m going to attempt to stay in the growth zone. Allowing myself to become excited and work up a level of anticipation to being stumped and feeling like I don’t quite know the answer, rather than a nervous resentment, which might have existed before.

Link to a resource that you found particularly useful or engaging.
Recommended watch (10 min)
The one that the course suggested has actually been the most engaging and helpful resource on this topic I have seen.

Reflect and discuss how these understandings may shape or influence your learning plan and strategy.
There is so much time left in the year, and I’m excited with the things I have learned to be put into moments that I’m able to practice the growth mindset and push myself to learn, rather than escape.
